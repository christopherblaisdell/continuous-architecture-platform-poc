# Simple Explanation — Real-Time Adventure Tracking

## The Problem

Right now, when a NovaTrek guest sets off on a glacier trek or canyon rafting adventure, nobody
back at base camp knows where they are. If someone gets hurt or lost, staff have to radio the
guide, figure out where the group last was, and only then decide where to send rescue. That whole
process can take 15 to 30 minutes — time that matters in a mountain emergency.

There is also a business problem. NovaTrek's insurance company requires the company to have GPS
records for all high-risk adventures by the end of this year, or risk losing coverage entirely.

## The Solution

We are building a live tracking system. Every guest wears a wristband that sends its GPS location
to NovaTrek's systems every few minutes. A safety officer sitting at the operations desk sees a
live map with dots showing where every adventure group is right now.

If a guest presses the SOS button on their wristband, or if their location drifts outside the
designated adventure area, the system automatically creates an emergency alert — no waiting for
a radio call. Rescue can be dispatched within minutes of the distress signal.

All of the location data is saved for at least 90 days. If there is ever a legal dispute or
insurance claim, NovaTrek has an exact GPS record of where everyone was and when.

## Why It Matters

This is both a safety improvement and a legal requirement. Faster rescue response directly saves
lives in remote wilderness environments. The GPS audit trail requirement is not optional — missing
it means losing the insurance policy that lets NovaTrek operate.
