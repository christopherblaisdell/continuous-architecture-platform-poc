## User Story

> As a park safety officer,
> I want real-time GPS tracking of all guests on active adventures with automated emergency alerting,
> So that rescue response times are reduced and insurance tracking mandates are met.

## Planned Capability Impact

| Capability | Impact | Description |
|-----------|--------|-------------|
| CAP-3.3 — Emergency Response Coordination | Enhanced | GPS-based emergency dispatch with real-time guest location |
| CAP-3.2 — Incident Reporting and Response | Enhanced | Automated incident creation from tracking anomalies and SOS signals |
| CAP-2.1 — Day-of-Adventure Check-In | Enhanced | Check-in completion triggers tracking session via wristband RFID |

## Acceptance Criteria (Derived)

1. GPS coordinates for all active adventure guests are available in near-real-time (latency under 30 seconds)
2. SOS signal from any guest device or wristband triggers an emergency alert within 10 seconds
3. Operations staff can view all active guest locations on a live map (wireframe: `live-tracking.excalidraw`)
4. Emergency alerts generate notifications to on-duty guides, dispatch team, and guest emergency contacts
5. Tracking sessions start automatically at check-in completion and end at adventure conclusion
6. All tracking and emergency events are audit-logged for insurance compliance (7-year retention)
7. Geofence violations (guest exits designated trail area) trigger automated warnings
8. System defaults to highest alert level for unknown tracking states (ADR-005 safety-first principle)
