## Summary

Real-Time Adventure Tracking and Emergency Alerting System

## User Story

As a park safety officer,
I want real-time GPS tracking of all guests on active adventures with automated emergency alerting,
So that rescue response times are reduced and insurance tracking mandates are met.

## Problem Statement

NovaTrek Adventures runs high-risk outdoor adventures (glacier treks, canyon rafting, alpine climbs)
with no real-time visibility into guest locations during active adventures. When an incident occurs:

1. **Rescue response is delayed** — staff must radio guides to establish last known position before
   dispatching rescue; average current delay is 15-30 minutes between incident and dispatch
2. **Insurance tracking mandates are unmet** — the company's liability insurer requires GPS audit
   trails for all Class 3 (Full Service) adventures as a condition of coverage renewal (deadline:
   end of calendar year)
3. **SOS has no automated pathway** — if a guest activates their wristband SOS signal, there is no
   system integration; the signal is logged only if a guide manually reports it
4. **No geofence enforcement** — guests who wander outside designated adventure boundaries are not
   detected until physically spotted by a guide or ranger

## Acceptance Criteria

1. A safety officer can view a live map showing current GPS positions of all active adventure parties
2. When a guest activates an SOS distress signal on their wristband, an emergency incident is
   automatically created in svc-emergency-response within 60 seconds of signal receipt
3. When a tracked device crosses a defined geofence boundary, an emergency alert is triggered
   automatically
4. Position telemetry is retained for a minimum of 90 days for insurance investigation purposes
5. svc-check-in activates a tracking session automatically when an adventure commences
6. Safety officers can retrieve the full position history for any session to support incident
   investigations
7. Wristband device authentication is separate from human user authentication
8. The system handles a minimum of 200 concurrent tracked adventures without degradation

## Business Impact

- Meets insurance GPS audit trail mandate (risk: coverage lapse without this)
- Reduces rescue dispatch delay from 15-30 minutes to under 2 minutes for automated triggers
- Enables proactive geofence alerting before guests are in danger
- Provides post-incident GPS evidence trail for liability defense
- Foundation for future guest-facing live tracking feature (guests sharing location with
  family members)
